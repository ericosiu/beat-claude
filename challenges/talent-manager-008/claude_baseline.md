## Building the People Function at Single Grain

### Executive Summary

Single Grain has no people function — just scattered hiring efforts by busy department heads. That's normal at 60 people. What's not normal is that the company runs on AI internally but applies none of it to how it finds, grows, and keeps its people. The fix: build an AI-native people function that treats every piece — recruiting, onboarding, performance, comp, retention — as parts of one integrated system, run by one person with AI as a force multiplier.

**90-Day Goal:** Stand up the full people infrastructure (ATS, structured hiring, onboarding program, comp bands, performance system), reduce time-to-fill from 45-60 days to 30, increase offer acceptance to 80%+, and push 90-day retention above 90%.

### The Full System

### Build Sequence Rationale

Recruiting first because there are 5-8 open roles bleeding revenue right now. Onboarding second because every hire made without it risks churning at 90 days. Comp third because the 65% offer acceptance rate suggests comp is a blocker. Performance fourth because you need people in seats before you can manage their performance. Retention fifth because it compounds — the earlier systems (better hiring, better onboarding, real comp) are themselves retention interventions.

## 3. Performance, Retention & Culture

### Performance Management System

**Not annual reviews. Not even quarterly reviews in the traditional sense.** At agency speed, waiting 90 days to give feedback is too slow.

**The System:**

| Cadence | What | Owner | AI Role |
|---------|------|-------|---------|
| Weekly | Manager 1:1 (30 min), structured template: wins, blockers, feedback both ways | Manager | AI generates 1:1 agenda from project data + previous notes |
| Monthly | Lightweight pulse check: 3 questions via Slack (energy level, clarity, support needed) | Me | AI analyzes trends, flags declining sentiment |
| Quarterly | Performance snapshot: manager writes 1-page assessment, employee writes self-review | Manager + employee | AI drafts starting point from 1:1 notes and project outcomes |
| Annually | Comp review + leveling assessment. Calibration across departments | Me + leadership | AI provides comp benchmarking data + performance summary |

**Handling Underperformance:**
- Week 1-2: Manager addresses in 1:1 with specific examples and expectations
- Week 3-4: If no improvement, formal 30-day performance plan with measurable goals
- Day 30: Clear decision — improved (stay) or not (part ways)
- Agency margins mean carrying underperformers is expensive. Kind, clear, and fast.

**Manager Enablement:**
- Managers are not HR people. Give them AI-powered tools: 1:1 question generators, feedback templates, difficult-conversation scripts
- Monthly "manager roundtable" (30 min) where managers share challenges and I coach
- AI reviews 1:1 notes for patterns: is feedback actually being given? Are the same issues recurring?

### Retention System

**The Early Warning Dashboard:**

| Signal | Source | Alert Threshold | Intervention |
|--------|--------|----------------|-------------|
| Engagement score drop | Monthly pulse survey | 2+ point decline over 2 months | Manager 1:1 + my check-in |
| 1:1 cancellations | Calendar data | 3+ cancellations in a month | Flag to manager's manager |
| LinkedIn activity spike | Manual/AI scan | Profile updated + activity increase | Proactive stay conversation |
| Tenure milestone | HRIS data | Approaching 12 months, 24 months | Career conversation + comp review |
| Manager relationship | Pulse survey | Low manager score + high overall | Consider team move |
| Burnout indicators | Pulse survey + PTO data | Low energy scores + no PTO taken | Mandatory time off discussion |

**Agency-Specific Retention Strategies:**
- **Client rotation:** Move team members across accounts every 12-18 months. Prevents burnout and builds broader skills
- **Career pathing:** Clear IC and management tracks so people see a future. Published internally with criteria
- **AI upskilling as retention:** Regular internal workshops on new AI tools. Position Single Grain as "the place where you become AI-fluent" — a skill so valuable people want to stay to keep learning
- **Comp competitiveness reviews:** AI-benchmarked quarterly, not annually. Don't let someone leave because you were 6 months late on a market adjustment
- **Stay interviews:** At 6 and 12 months, proactively ask: "What keeps you here? What would make you leave?" Don't wait for the exit interview

### Culture Strategy: Bridging Agency and TES

The agency-to-TES transition creates cultural tension. Agency people value client relationships, creative work, and collaborative energy. Product/TES people value shipping, iteration, and metrics. Both are right for their context.

**The Bridge:**
- **Unified values, different work styles.** Core values (AI-forward, speed, ownership, transparency) apply everywhere. How work gets done can differ by team
- **Cross-pollination projects.** Agency people work on product features. Product people sit in on client calls. Build empathy across the divide
- **Shared wins.** Celebrate agency client wins and product milestones with equal visibility. One Slack channel, one all-hands format
- **Unified leveling.** Same career framework for both sides. An L3 on the agency side and an L3 on the product side have equivalent scope and comp — just different work

## Summary

Single Grain doesn't just need a recruiter — it needs a people function built from scratch. The strategy prioritizes:

1. **Recruiting infrastructure first** (ATS, process, pipeline) because open roles are bleeding revenue now
2. **Onboarding and comp next** because every hire without them risks churning or rejecting the offer
3. **Performance and retention systems last** because they compound — but only after you have people in seats and the infrastructure to support them

The AI-native approach means one person can run the entire function at a level that would normally require a 3-person team: AI handles sourcing, screening, scheduling, analytics, comp benchmarking, and sentiment analysis. Humans handle judgment calls — who to hire, how to coach, when to intervene, and the relationship work that keeps people engaged.

The agency-to-TES transition is the hardest cultural challenge. The people function has to be the bridge — unified leveling, shared values, cross-pollination — so that agency veterans and product builders feel like they're on the same team, even when the work looks different.
